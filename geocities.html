News -- July 13, 2026

Coup D'etat 7" Lathe Released!

Coup D'etat just released their self-titled promo EP on a black 7" lathe cut, limited to 50 copies total. 30 of those went straight to the band, which leaves ZBR with just 20 to sell -- and lathes never last long once they're posted. Two re-recorded songs and a brand new one round out the tracklist, marking the band's peak to date with "new" guitarist Elle and the last recording to feature OG bassist Raine.

These are short, sharp, seething jams that clock in fast and leave a mark. If you've been following the band through the Zampler comps, this is your chance to grab their material on an actual physical format before it's gone. The band's touring on this one too, so their personal copies will likely disappear at merch tables before we even sell through ours.

Once these are gone, they're gone -- lathes don't get repressed.

Releases -- July 3, 2026

Zampler #27 Released

ZAMPLER #27, 'Take It Out Easy, Cadet,' dropped on June 30th and it's a big one -- 25 songs deep, with a stacked chunk of previously unreleased material from Senza, Our Future Is An Absolute Shadow, Poetry Of Torch, Coup D'etat, The Names Of Our Friends, Hauzu, Empathetic, and Mourn Recif.

As always, it's housed on cdr in the traditional Zampler packaging, and it's basically made-to-order -- place any order over $20 anytime and one gets thrown in automatically. No need to hunt for a separate listing, it just happens at checkout.

If you've collected any of the previous 26 installments you already know the drill: this is the cheapest, weirdest way to hear a snapshot of the whole roster at once.
